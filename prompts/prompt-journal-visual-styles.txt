========================================
JOURNAL VISUAL STYLES (ALL IMPORT PROFILES)
========================================

This section governs **optional post-JSON image generation** in the chat workflow (after valid JSON exists and the user explicitly confirms each image). It does **not** tell you how to fill `narrativecard.imagetitle` inside JSON — that field is a separate, short **UI caption** for the journal page (see the IMPORT PROFILE).

There is **no** `imageprompt` key in the import schema. Rich generation prompts belong only in the follow-up conversation when you generate an image, built from card text + these rules — not stuffed into JSON fields.

Generate images only when the profile allows it, the JSON is accepted, and the user has explicitly confirmed each image (see POST-JSON IMAGE WORKFLOW in the journal core above).

Generate an image of the described subject for a tabletop RPG campaign.

The subject must dominate the frame and be the clear focal point of the composition.


--------------------------------
STYLE RULES (ALWAYS ENFORCED) — ALL IMAGES
--------------------------------

**Visual target (shared):** grounded **cinematic fantasy realism** — painterly but **restrained**, atmospheric, believable — closer to a still from a **grounded historical / fantasy drama** than to epic splash art, glossy game key art, or heavy graphic-novel linework. When in doubt, **quiet tension and lived-in believability** over spectacle.

Rendering:
- **Painterly realism** with **subtle** brush texture; **minimal** visible graphic linework — avoid exaggerated strokes, noisy surfaces, and over-illustrated “concept art clutter.”
- **Selective detail** and **clear focal hierarchy**: one primary read; support the focal subject with quieter surroundings. **Readability over density** — do not fill every inch with competing ornament.
- Avoid **visual clutter**, overcrowding, and ornamental noise outside the focal area; **restrained environmental detail** where the eye should rest.
- **No photoreal** hyper-sharp DSLR snapshot look; also **no** hyper-polished cinematic **fantasy-poster** sheen or heroic “splash art” drift.

Figures (when any appear):
- **Naturalistic staging**: people **embedded** in the space with believable posture and weight — not a posed lineup, not fashion-catalog heroes.
- Prefer **believable social behavior**: small clusters, isolated conversations, averted or wary glances, exhaustion, hands busy with ordinary tasks — **behavioral storytelling**, not mannequins.

Lighting (shared baseline):
- **Practical or motivated light** where it makes sense; **atmospheric** falloff and **softened contrast** — restrained value range, not high-contrast “concept art grading.”
- Gentle warm rim or edge read only when motivated (for portraits, face and hair can catch a soft rim — still not hard comic ink).

Palette:
- Muted earthy tones with warm ochres, browns, and desaturated neutrals unless a specific color is narratively driven.

Art direction:
- Fantasy setting, **dramatic but grounded**; **believable materials** and wear.
- **Single cohesive image**; one clear story beat.
- No grids, panels, sheets, or collages.

Portrait and illustration both use the block above; the PORTRAIT and ILLUSTRATION sections below add bucket-specific composition (they override where they conflict).


--------------------------------
ILLUSTRATION (SCENE / ENVIRONMENT / PLACE / OBJECT)
--------------------------------

Use this bucket when the narrativecard subject is primarily a place, crowd, interior, street, landscape, landmark, or a non-portrait object in context (shop, room, city block, clearing, wagon, map table, artifact as focal prop, etc.) — not a dedicated NPC portrait (see PORTRAIT).

The subject may be:
- A scene or environment
- A single object or artifact

The subject must dominate the frame and be the clear focal point of the composition.

ASPECT RATIO RULES (illustration)

If the subject is a SCENE or ENVIRONMENT:
- Landscape aspect ratio (16x9)
- Landscape canvas only
- The environment must fill the entire frame

If the subject is an OBJECT or ARTIFACT:
- Use square (1:1) unless otherwise specified
- Object must dominate the frame

FRAMING RULES (illustration)

- Eye-level perspective
- Naturalistic frontal or forward-facing composition appropriate to the subject
- No extreme angles
- No top-down or bird's-eye views
- No dramatic tilt
- Avoid unnecessary zoom-out or distant pull-back
- The image must feel intentional and composed

Scene-specific composition:
- Allow environmental depth
- Avoid panoramic emptiness
- Anchor composition around a strong central structural element

Human behavior (when the scene includes people — not a pure empty exterior):
- **Naturalistic social staging**: patrons leaning in for a private word, distracted glances, isolated pairs, someone turned away, guarded posture, tired hands on a tankard — **quiet behavioral storytelling**, not a posed cast grid.
- Avoid "everyone looking at camera" or synchronized heroic gestures.

Object-specific composition:
- Tight framing
- Object centered and clearly visible
- Minimal background distraction

Do not put text or UI in the frame.

When generating an image in the post-JSON step for an **illustration** card: build the full prompt from the card's title, description, and scene context, applying the rules in this ILLUSTRATION section and STYLE RULES above. The JSON `imagetitle` is at most a short human label echo — not a substitute for a full prompt.


--------------------------------
PORTRAIT (CHARACTER / NPC)
--------------------------------

Use this bucket when the narrativecard subject is a specific named person (key NPC, portrait card). This is a different composition contract than ILLUSTRATION: tight, straight-on tabletop character portrait — not a cinematic environmental shot, not mid-body adventure art.

Context: Dungeons & Dragons (or compatible fantasy RPG) NPC portrait.

STRUCTURED FACETS (derive from the character; use when composing the **post-JSON portrait generation prompt** in chat — never pack these into JSON `imagetitle`)

Populate from the journal JSON / scene context where known; use reasonable inference or leave class empty if unknown:
- NAME — character name
- CREATURE RACE — e.g. Human, Elf, Dwarf
- CREATURE CLASS — e.g. Fighter, Wizard; empty string if unknown or not applicable
- GENDER — as given or inferred neutrally from description
- EXPRESSION — mood in a few words (e.g. guarded, intelligent, slightly suspicious)
- PROP — notable held or worn prop, or "None"
- HAIR — color, style, length
- AGE — apparent age band
- SKIN — tone and notable features (weathering, scars, etc.)
- PHYSIQUE — build and notable facial structure

CORE ASK (portrait generation)

Generate a tight, straight-on character portrait of a [CREATURE RACE] [CREATURE CLASS], [GENDER], fantasy RPG NPC, facing directly toward the camera with a [EXPRESSION] expression. Eye-level view, frontal perspective only.

IMPORTANT COMPOSITION RULES (portrait — overrides generic "character framing" elsewhere in this file)

- Extreme close portrait framing.
- The face must dominate the frame.
- Crop at the shoulders only.
- Top of head may approach the upper frame edge.
- No torso visible below upper chest.
- No waist, hips, or full arms.
- No dynamic pose.
- Character centered in frame.
- Looking directly toward camera.
- Portrait should feel intimate and confrontational, not cinematic or environmental.

PROP RULES

- If PROP is specified, include it only as a cropped partial element near the edge of frame.
- Prop must never dominate composition.
- Prop must never obscure the face.
- Hands may appear only partially and cropped tightly.

PHYSICAL DETAIL

Apply HAIR, AGE, SKIN, and PHYSIQUE as subtle controlled adjustments only. Do not alter framing or camera angle.

PORTRAIT RENDERING (extends STYLE RULES above — face-specific)

Painterly **realism** on the face: readable structure and expression with **restrained** surface texture — avoid noisy cross-hatching, heavy comic outlines, or over-worked brush noise.

Soft directional light on the face and hair with **gentle** falloff; **moderate** contrast — not hard HDR or high-key concept-art crunch.

Muted earthy palette with warm ochres, browns, desaturated reds, and smoky neutrals.

Semi-realistic proportions with **subtle** stylization only where it aids expression. Expressive eyes without exaggeration. Facial asymmetry and small imperfections welcome. No plastic skin; no photoreal pores-for-pores snapshot look.

BACKGROUND (portrait)

Simple unobtrusive background with soft gradients and faint atmospheric particles only. Background must not contain scenery, architecture, props, or storytelling elements.

CLOSING TAGS

Fantasy concept art, tabletop RPG portrait, dramatic mood, **selective detail on the face**, sharp focus on eyes and expression, square composition.

ASPECT RATIO (portrait)

- Square aspect ratio (1:1)
- Square canvas only
- Single character only

When generating an image in the post-JSON step for a **portrait** card: build the full prompt using the STRUCTURED FACETS above plus narrativecard title/description and conversation context, applying PORTRAIT rules and STYLE RULES. The JSON `imagetitle` remains a short UI caption only — not a container for facets or generation instructions.


--------------------------------
FAILURE LINE
--------------------------------

If image generation fails, output exactly:
Image generation failed

If the model cannot generate the image for any reason, it should clearly say: "Image generation failed", not describe it.
